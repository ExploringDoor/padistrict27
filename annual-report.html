A Letter from the District Administrator

Dear District 27 Family,

2026 was the year District 27 stopped guessing. For the first time in our history, every game played in our 12 chartered leagues — across three counties — was tracked, scored, and remembered in one place. Ten thousand kids took the field this season. Forty-two umpires called balls and strikes. Eighteen hundred volunteers signed up to keep our complexes open, our snack bars stocked, and our score books moving.

This report isn't about wins and losses. It's about what we did with $214 thousand in sponsor gifts, what 22,000 volunteer hours look like in practice, and how we kept our promise to the Scott family that no D27 kid would ever miss a season because their family couldn't afford it.

You'll see numbers. You'll see names. You'll see a few quotes from coaches and parents that almost made me cry when I read them. But mostly, you'll see what one of America's largest Little League districts looks like when it's working — and what it's pointed at next.

Thank you. For the time, the money, the snack-bar shifts, the field drags before sunrise. For the kids who showed up scared and went home tall. For all of it.

Play ball.

Jeff Bennett

District Administrator · PA District 27 Little League

Three Stories From This Season

Berwyn-Paoli's Bob Jones run

For the first time in twelve years, Berwyn-Paoli Red 12U won the Bob Jones Tournament — sweeping the bracket from the #1 seed without dropping a game. Coach Tom Caldwell's three-year rebuild paid off in front of 800 parents at Lower Merion Field 3. The deciding hit: a Luke DiSanto two-run double in the bottom of the fifth.

"We weren't supposed to be here. The town wasn't ready for it. But these twelve kids and their families showed up every Tuesday and Thursday night for three years. Some nights it was 38 degrees. Tonight is for them."
— Tom Caldwell, BP Red 12U Manager · post-game interview

The first family on a full scholarship

This year, the Scott Memorial Scholarship Fund covered every fee for one D27 family of three across the entire summer — registration, equipment, uniforms, travel-team fees, and the All-Star tournament path. The family asked to remain anonymous. The scholarship chair received a hand-written letter on opening day: "You gave them their summer. We don't know how to thank you."

Mike Russo's 22 years

At the October banquet, we inducted Mike Russo into the D27 Hall of Fame. For 22 years, every Saturday morning at 6 AM, Marple Newtown's fields were ready because of Mike. The drag, the lines, the lights. He showed up before sunrise even when his own kids had aged out. "I just like the way the fields look at 7 AM," he said, when we asked why.

What's Next · 2027 & Beyond

We're moving the District operations onto a real production platform — built and maintained in-house at zero development cost. The pitch demo at padistrict27.vercel.app shows the design. Phase 1 launch begins June 2027 if approved by the board this winter. Operating cost: roughly ⅓ of what 12 leagues already pay for Sports Connect.

For 2027 specifically:

  • Open Spring 2027 registration on the new system Nov 15, 2026
  • Pilot Phase 1 with one league (Berwyn-Paoli proposed) for full season
  • Live broadcast at least 4 D27 tournament games via partner high schools
  • Hit $50K in sponsor commitments by March 1 (currently $48K · 96%)
  • Bring our umpire pool from 42 to 60
  • Expand the Scott Scholarship endowment to $300K
  • Induct Class of 2027 to the Hall of Fame
