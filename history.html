Our History

The people and traditions behind District 27's signature tournaments — names that have stuck for generations.

PA District 27 Little League provides leadership and guidance to locally chartered Little Leagues across Chester, Delaware, and Montgomery counties — supporting thousands of baseball and softball players each season. Several of the District's tournaments carry the names of volunteers who shaped the game here long before District 27 existed.

Special Games · Major League Team Tournament

The Bob Jones Tournament

Bob Jones served as President of Bridgeport Little League until his death while in office. To honor him, Tony Marra renamed the old "First Place Tournament" in his memory. When District 27 was established, the tournament kept the name — and even after Bridgeport relocated to District 22 and it formally became the Major League "Team" Tournament, "the name still sticks."

Unlike an all-star event, the Bob Jones is an invitational for regular-season Major League teams (ages 11–12) playing on 46/60 fields. Entire teams compete — no player substitution or sharing — so it includes players of all skill levels, giving kids who might never make an all-star roster the chance to represent their league.

Special Games · District Invitational

The John Klein Tournament

Before District 27 formed, many of its leagues belonged to the old District 23. John Klein was a dedicated volunteer from Chester Valley Little League who was the backbone of the "B" tournament — managing multiple fields at once at the Chester Valley VFW and Monument Avenue complexes.

Before his passing, District 23 Administrator Tony Marra presented Klein with a banner honoring the tournament that now bears his name.

Established 2001 · 8–10 Year Old International

Al “Pappy” Patton Memorial Tournament

Established in 2001, this tournament honors Al "Pappy" Patton and his 62 years of service to Little League as an umpire. Patton served as Assistant District Administrator under Tony Marra for both District 23 and District 27.

In 1957, Patton was assigned as first-base umpire for the Williamsport World Series — the game in which the first and only perfect game in Little League World Series Championship history was pitched, the event that inspired the 2009 film The Perfect Game. Patton was inducted into the PA Sports Hall of Fame in 1984.
