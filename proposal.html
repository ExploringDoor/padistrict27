A 1-page summary of what it takes to turn padistrict27.vercel.app into the operational backbone of D27 — and what it costs.

D27

The Opportunity

The demo at padistrict27.vercel.app shows 50 pages — portals, brackets, schedules, scholarship, sponsors, store, and operations tools that don't exist anywhere else in Little League. Jeff: it works as a demo. To run D27 on it, we need eight specific Phase-1 features wired to real systems. This document defines those features, the timeline, and the high-level budget.

D27 currently operates on email chains, group texts, paper pitch affidavits, and a PDF-list website. Production migration replaces all of it.

Phase 1 — Launch Scope (8 deliverables)

  • 1. Authentication & roles — Firebase Auth · magic-link login · role detection (Admin / President / Captain / Player).
  • 2. Database migration — Firestore · all 12 leagues · rosters · games · pitch counts · communications.
  • 3. Sports Connect bridge — nightly roster sync from SC (LL-mandated registration system).
  • 4. GameChanger PDF import — captains drag-drop PDF after game; system parses + publishes.
  • 5. Stripe Connect — registration fees · donations · sponsor invoices · swag store · per-league split.
  • 6. Email + SMS broadcasting — SendGrid + Twilio · rainouts, RSVP reminders, cert lapse alerts.
  • 7. JDP / Safesport / Concussion API — auto-mark coaches cleared/expired (currently manual).
  • 8. ICS calendar feeds — subscribe links auto-update Google / Apple / Outlook calendars.

For comparison: Sports Connect Premium runs ~$400/league/year × 12 = $4,800/yr and only covers registration. D27's all-in cost is ⅓ of SC alone — and includes everything beyond it.

Revenue Offsets

  • Swag store — projected ~$8K/yr profit at 5% adoption · 50% to leagues / 50% to scholarship.
  • Tournament hosting fees — $400 × 8 outside teams / yr = $3,200 currently mailed in by check.
  • Sponsor analytics retention — current 12% sponsor churn drops to ~5% with ROI dashboards.
  • Scholarship donations — projected 3× lift with on-site donate button (currently $214K total since 2008).

Why Now

Sports Connect is registration-only. GameChanger doesn't talk across teams. TeamSnap doesn't handle districts. No platform exists for what a district administrator actually does. D27 has the chance to be first — and to set the template for the other 511 LL districts.

The Ask

Three paths forward. Pick one:

Option A · Phase 1 Launch (Recommended)
Greenlight 6-week build → live July 15 with one league + Bob Jones tournament on production. Full migration by season's end. Cost: TBD dev · ~$1,400/yr ops.
Option B · Pilot League Only
Pick one league (BP suggested). Run them end-to-end on production for Spring 2027. Other 11 leagues stay on demo. Cost: TBD dev · ~$400/yr ops for one league.
Option C · Phase 0 Continuation
Keep the demo as a public-facing brochure. Refine quarterly. No backend, no Phase 1. Cost: ~$0 / yr.

Recommendation: Option A. Operating costs are ⅓ of what 12 leagues already pay for Sports Connect. Risk is contained — fall back to demo at any time. Upside: D27 becomes the platform other districts call about. Phase 1 build cost to be discussed.
