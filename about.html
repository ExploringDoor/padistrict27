About the District

One Team.
One Little League.

PA District 27 was chartered in 1993 when District 23 grew too large to serve its leagues. Thirty-plus years later, we're still the umbrella — 12 member leagues, 10,000 kids, three counties, every summer.

Our Purpose

Why we do this

What the District is for

Every one of our 12 leagues runs its own season — fields, uniforms, schedules, volunteer boards. The District exists to do the things that only make sense at the umbrella level: run All-Star and Section-qualifying tournaments, hold umpires to one standard, advocate for the leagues with Little League International, and quietly clean up the messes before they ever reach a parent's inbox.

What we commit to every season

  • 17 tournaments — baseball & softball, 8-year-olds through 16
  • Every District game umpired by a certified volunteer crew
  • A standing annual scholarship for graduating D27 players
  • A public schedule every team can plan around by April 1
  • One set of playing rules, consistently applied, across all 12 leagues

A Brief History

How we got here
1957
A District 23 umpire at the LLWS
Al "Pappy" Patton umpires first base at the Little League World Series Championship — the first year a non-US team competed (Monterrey, Mexico) and the only perfect game ever thrown in the LLWS Championship. That Series, and the people who volunteered around it, became the template for how this district thinks about the game.
1984
Pappy inducted, PA Sports Hall of Fame
62 years behind the plate. Founding member of the Little League World Series Umpires Alumni International.
1993
District 23 splits — District 27 is chartered
D23 had grown too large to serve every league well. Under Tony Marra, the district split along geography. Van Scott — a Pottstown LL founder who had served as Tony's ADA — was named the first Administrator of PA District 27. His wife Laura Scott took on Financial Coordinator. The patch every D27 player wears on their sleeve goes back to that year.
2001
Pappy passes · BB-10 renamed
The 8-9-10 YO baseball tournament is renamed the Al "Pappy" Patton Memorial. It still runs every June at a different host complex, voted on by the 12 leagues.
2004
Van & Laura step down
After 11 years they step out of district roles. The next year, the Laura & Van Scott Higher Education Scholarship is endowed in their name — awarded annually at the October banquet to graduating players pursuing post-secondary education.
Today
12 leagues, still one district
The 2026 season marks 33 years of PA District 27. Jeff Bennett serves as District Administrator, Jason Saylor as ADA. This site is an open proposal to give the District what a good umbrella deserves: one place parents, coaches, and umps can count on.

Honored In Our Tournaments

The people our tournaments are named for
BJ

Bob Jones

President · Bridgeport LL
Died in office

Bob gave decades to youth baseball as president of Bridgeport Little League. He died while still serving. Tony Marra — who ran the old D23 — named the district's marquee Majors tournament in his honor. The Bob Jones Tournament is single-elimination: one loss and you're done. The bracket every June carries his name forward.

JK

John Klein

Volunteer · Chester Valley LL
Mainstay of the old "B" tournament

John was at Chester Valley's VFW and Monument Avenue fields for what feels like every weekend of every summer for years. Before he passed, Tony Marra presented him with a banner and told him that from now on, the "B" tournament would carry his name. It runs every year at CV's complex — the same fields John worked.

PP

Al "Pappy" Patton

ADA D23 & D27 · 62-year umpire
1957 LLWS Championship, 1st base

Pappy umpired the 1957 Little League World Series Championship — the first year a non-US team competed (Monterrey, Mexico) and still the only perfect game ever pitched in an LLWS final. That game inspired the 2009 film The Perfect Game. Founding member of the LLWS Umpires Alumni International. Inducted into the PA Sports Hall of Fame in 1984. He was at home plate in this district longer than most of us have been alive.
