Tournament Rules & Guidelines

Everything managers, umpires, and parents need for the International Tournament season — age eligibility, pitch counts, mercy rule, waivers, and residency verification. Consolidated from Little League International regulations and D27-specific procedures.

Last updated · Apr 2026 · 2026 season

At a Glance

The five rules that most often decide close games. Full Little League rulebook governs everything not listed here.

★ Most Missed

Batting Order

Tournament teams bat their entire lineup (continuous order). A player who leaves the game may re-enter. Mandatory Play rules apply — every rostered player must get at least one at-bat and six defensive outs.

Pitching

One-Day Rest Rule

A 11–12U pitcher who throws 41+ pitches in a day requires 1 calendar day of rest. 66+ pitches requires 3 days. No pitcher may catch in the same game after being removed.

End of Game

Mercy Rule

Majors & Juniors: game ends if a team leads by 10 after 4 complete innings (or 3½ if home). 15-run rule after 3 innings. Intermediate: 10 after 4; 15 after 3.

Lineup

Mandatory Play

Every rostered player present must receive a minimum of one at-bat and play six consecutive defensive outs. Penalty for violation: protest may be upheld and game forfeited.

Eligibility

Residency & School

Players must live within league boundaries OR attend school within boundaries. Proof required via 3-document residency test OR school enrollment form. Exceptions require waiver.

Uniform

Jewelry & Equipment

No jewelry (medical alert excepted). Catchers must wear full gear including dangling throat guard on mask. Pitchers may not wear white/gray sleeves. USA-marked bats required for Majors and below.

Same-Day Exception

If a pitcher reaches their daily limit while a batter is in the box, the pitcher may continue facing that batter only. Any pitch over the daily maximum otherwise forfeits pitching eligibility for the rest of the game AND subjects the team to potential protest.

Residency & School Attendance

A player is eligible for a chartered league if they live OR attend school within that league's boundary. Both are established through documentation on file before tournament rosters are submitted.

Residency

3-Document Proof

Parent/guardian must submit three documents from three separate groups (driver's license, utility bill, tax form, lease, etc.) all dated within Feb 1 – Feb 1 prior and after, showing same address inside league boundary.

School

Enrollment Form

Alternatively, a School Enrollment Form signed by the school (principal or registrar) covering the full school year just completed, showing the school is inside league boundary.

Pre-Season Deadline

All residency documentation must be on file with the league and audited by the District Administrator before the International Tournament roster is submitted (approximately June 1). Late submissions invalidate tournament eligibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers for the calls Jeff and Jason get most every tournament week.

Can a player be added to the tournament roster mid-tournament?
No. Tournament rosters are locked when submitted to Williamsport (typically June 1). The only exception is a pre-submitted player returning from an excused absence (documented illness, injury, family emergency). New additions are not permitted.
What happens if a pitcher hits their pitch limit mid-batter?
The pitcher may continue facing the current batter to completion. Once the plate appearance ends, they must be removed. They may return to the game at another position but may not pitch again that day.
Can a player pitch and catch in the same game?
Yes, but with restrictions. A player who caught 4+ innings may not pitch the same day. A pitcher who threw 41+ pitches may not catch the remainder of the day. This is tracked automatically on the D27 live scorer.
Our league had a rainout — how do we reschedule?
Contact the Tournament Director (listed on the bracket page) within 24 hours. Rain make-ups follow the published alternate-date schedule. Field availability is coordinated through the D27 field scheduler.
Where does the umpire come from for tournament games?
All D27 tournament umpires are certified through the District 27 Umpire-in-Chief program (UIC: Frank Nikischer). Crews are assigned by UIC no later than 48 hours before first pitch.
What's the protest procedure?
Protests of rule interpretations must be lodged at the time of the incident, before the next pitch. Judgment calls are not protestable. The Tournament Director rules on the spot. Formal written protest goes to the DA within 24 hours.
Does District 27 follow Little League International rules exactly?
Yes — D27 does not modify tournament playing rules. Local procedures (scheduling, make-up dates, umpire assignment) are D27-specific but baseline rules defer to the current-year Little League rulebook.
